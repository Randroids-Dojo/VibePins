Open Questions

Questions here block or influence implementation.

Critical convention. Every question must include a <dt>Recommended default</dt> entry. The loop ships under that default and leaves the question open for override. Do not block the loop on dev sign-off. Stress-tested values that survive multiple iterations get frozen.

Q-032: Pin body damping vs collider reshape to settle a tethered toppled pin

Context
Playtest bug 4: after a reset, one pin kept swinging and never settled. Root cause (found with a deterministic real-Rapier 60fps test): a pin collider is a plain cylinder, so a knocked-down pin lying on its side is a perfect roller, and with its neck still on its cord the cord's restoring pull plus the belly contact drive a tetherball-style limit cycle. The pin spins about its long axis indefinitely and never reaches the at-rest thresholds. The cord is the driver (the same toppled pin with no cord decays to rest), so damping must be large enough to win against the cord pumping the spin.
Options
  1. Modest body damping on the pins (pinLinearDamping 0.4, pinAngularDamping 6.0), modelling the energy a real duckpin (belly plus a flat foot, not a true cylinder) sheds into the deck. Smallest change; a topple lasts well under a second so the per-step decay barely touches the lively topple/scatter.
  2. Reshape the pin collider away from a plain cylinder (a compound belly + foot, or a capsule) so it is no longer a perfect roller. More physically faithful but a much larger change that re-tunes the whole scatter / cord / detection physics.
Recommended default
A. Damping is the smallest change that reliably settles a tethered toppled pin, and the scatter smoke (which builds its own undamped rack) confirms the duckpin spray is unaffected. Shipped under A. Revisit B only if a later playtest shows the damping deadening the topple feel.
Owner
Playtest gate (Q-010).

Q-029: Ball launch topspin (carry-through vs strike rarity)

Context
Playtest bug 4: on a decent pocket hit the ball stopped dead at the head pin and only the front pin fell, instead of carrying through into the pins behind. The real-physics root cause was the launch forward roll: Ball.launch() set a full rolling-without-slip topspin (omega_x = v / r). When that topspinning ball met the head pin it gripped the pin's side and CLIMBED it, popping the ball roughly 0.8m into the air so it sailed over the whole rack and clipped only a pin or two on the way down. Friction, restitution, ball mass, and pin mass were all swept in real Rapier and none fixed the pop-up; the topspin was the dominant driver. Reducing it lets the ball drive THROUGH the pocket low (peak height roughly 0.5m) and carry into the row behind. A real ball also still carries skid at the pocket rather than perfect roll, so a fraction of pure roll is the physical reading. Open tunable: the launch topspin fraction LANE.ballLaunchTopspin. PIN_PHYSICS (friction 0.55, restitution 0) and the belly-heavy mass are deliberately UNCHANGED so the carefully tuned REQ-030 scatter material stands.
Options
  1. ballLaunchTopspin = 0.5 (half of pure rolling-without-slip). Real-Rapier sweep across representative pocket throws: the ball drives low through the head pin (over-deck peak roughly 0.5m, well under pin-fly-over height) and carries into a pin behind on a healthy majority of throws, while a dead-centre straight ball still never strikes and no representative pocket throw clears the whole rack. The visible down-lane roll is essentially unchanged (the ball is on the bed either way and bed friction is low enough that the exact launch spin barely moves the visible roll).
  2. Keep full topspin (1.0): the over-damped behaviour the playtest rejected (the ball flies over the rack, only the front pin falls).
  3. Drop topspin further toward 0: the ball drives through even lower but a straight ball then stalls and topples only the head pin, losing carry.
Recommended default
A. ballLaunchTopspin = 0.5. It is the simplest single-tunable fix (RULE 7), it leaves the REQ-030 scatter material untouched, it reliably carries a good pocket hit past the head pin into the pack, and it keeps duckpin strikes hard (no straight strike, no representative pocket strike). Verified by tests/carry-through.smoke.test.ts, which fails against option B and passes under A. First-pass value; the product owner can re-playtest and nudge it.

Q-012: Foul scoring and rack handling

Context
Foul-line detection (REQ-032) flags an over-the-line release: the ball is at or in front of the foul line plane (z = 0, the approach side) while live. The detector exists; the question is how a foul scores. In sanctioned bowling a foul counts the ball as zero, and any pins the fouling ball knocked are reset (the rack is restored to before the throw), so the foul costs the throw but not the rack.
Options
  1. Foul scores zero pinfall for that ball and the rack is restored to its pre-ball standing state. Pins the dead ball disturbed are reeled back by the between-balls reset, so the player still has the same pins to clear on the next ball (or the next frame if it was the third ball). Matches sanctioned play.
  2. Foul scores zero but any pins the ball physically knocked stay down (no restoration), so a foul can still "help" by clearing pins for free. Simpler to implement but rewards fouling.

Recommended default
A. It matches universal bowling convention (a foul is a dead ball, zero, rack restored) and stays consistent with the existing zero-count ball path in the Game spine (a foul is recorded as recordBall(0), and the returned between-balls reset reels any disturbed pins back to their home spots). This decides whether an input like a tenth frame of [10, 7, 6] is legal (independent balls, score 23) or impossible (7 + 6 = 13 on a shared rack). It directly affects server-side rejection of impossible scores (REQ-059).
Options

A tenth-frame spare's single bonus ball is on a fresh rack (0-10).
  2. Every bonus ball on its own fresh rack: each bonus ball is an independent 0-10 regardless of the previous bonus ball, so [10, 7, 6] would be legal.
Recommended default
A. This matches universal tenpin/duckpin tenth-frame convention and the most natural reading of "re-rack as needed" (the re-rack happens when a strike clears the deck, not unconditionally). The scoring engine validates under A: a tenth-frame strike's two bonus balls obey bonus1 + bonus2 <= 10 unless bonus1 is itself a strike. Shipped under A; flip to B here if a target duckpin house actually re-racks between every bonus ball.

Q-018: Between-balls string motion, lift fallen vs recall all

Context
A playtest dot described the between-balls reset as "recall (lift) all pins, then lower only the standing ones, leaving fallen cleared".   1. Lift only the fallen pins clear of the deck and leave them aloft; never touch the standing pins. Matches the old GDD wording and is the simplest motion.
  2. Recall (lift) all ten, then lower only the standing pins back onto their home spots, leaving the downed pins aloft. Matches a real string machine and the playtester's observation at Pins Mechanical.
Recommended default

Status
resolved
Resolution
